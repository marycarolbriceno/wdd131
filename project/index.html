Each year, only around 10% of the large homeless animal population is ever admitted to an animal shelter

Shelters work tirelessly to rescue animals from tough situations, offering them safety and medical care. However, space and resources are limited. By adopting from a shelter, you not only give that animal a loving home, but also free up space for the shelter to rescue more animals in need.

Homeless animals face numerous challenges in their daily lives

They struggle to find basic necessities like food, water, shelter, and are more vulnerable to diseases, harsh weather, and human cruelty.
